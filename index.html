BDay by day

Morning and afternoon, with a food pick and a quick tip for each.

  1. 01
    Day 01Fri · Jun 19 · Land easy
    AM

    Central Park — south-end amble. The Pond, Gapstow Bridge, Sheep Meadow and the Mall.Gapstow Bridge has the classic skyline-over-the-water view. Keep landing day gentle — it's all a short walk from Park Lane.

    EatBouchon Bakery · Columbus Circle.The 3rd-floor café overlooks the park — go for the croissants and the view.

    PM

    Fifth Avenue & Rockefeller Center. An easy stroll past St Patrick's and the flagship shops.If you've got the legs, book Top of the Rock for ~1 hr before sunset to catch day, dusk and the lights.

    EatKeens Steakhouse · Midtown.Book ahead, or walk into the pub room for the same menu without a reservation — the mutton chop is the move.

  2. 02
    Day 02Sat · Jun 20 · Harbour & the old city
    AM

    Statue of Liberty & Ellis Island. Earliest ferry from Battery Park.Book online and take the first slot to beat heat and queues; Ellis Island's immigration museum is the real depth — don't skip it.

    EatBlack Fox Coffee · FiDi.Top drip coffee and a solid breakfast a few blocks from the terminal — fuel up before boarding.

    PM

    9/11 Memorial & Museum, then Wall Street, the Charging Bull and the NYSE.Allow ~2 hrs; much of it is photo-free and quietly intense. Short on time? The free Staten Island Ferry gives a Liberty pass-by so you can come straight here.

    EatThe Dead Rabbit · FiDi.Reserve the upstairs parlour for a calmer sit-down; the Irish coffee is the signature.

  3. 03
    Day 03Sun · Jun 21 · Museum mile
    AM

    The Met. Arrive at the 10am opening.Beeline to your top wing before the crowds — Egyptian, European Paintings, the rooftop. Tickets ~US$30, book online. (Closed Wednesdays, which is why it's on Sunday.)

    EatTompkins Square Bagels · Upper East Side.A proper New York bagel — get it with lox and a schmear for the full breakfast.

    PM

    Central Park's lake, from the Met's back door — Bethesda Fountain, Bow Bridge, rowboats at Loeb Boathouse.Exit the Met west straight into the park. Prefer indoors? The Natural History Museum is a 10-min walk across.

    EatLa Pecora Bianca · Upper East Side.Bright modern Italian with outdoor tables — the whipped ricotta toast and fresh pasta. A relaxed late lunch.

  4. 04
    Day 04 · Mon · Jun 22Match day
    AM

    Chelsea Market + the High Line — walk the elevated park north toward Hudson Yards.Chelsea Market is a food hall in an old Nabisco factory; the High Line entrance is right beside it.

    EatChelsea Market food hallGraze — the original Los Tacos No.1 stand, a lobster roll at The Lobster Place, or Friedman's for brunch.

    PM

    Hudson Yards — the Edge sky deck or the Vessel, then to Penn Station.Hudson Yards is right by Penn, so you're perfectly placed for the train. Keep the afternoon easy — big night ahead.

    EatJongro BBQ · KoreatownGrill-at-the-table KBBQ, two blocks from Penn — eat well, stadium food lines are long.

  5. 05
    Day 05Tue · Jun 23 · Brooklyn & a fan zone
    AM

    Walk the Brooklyn Bridge from the Manhattan side into DUMBO.Go before ~9–10am to beat crowds and heat; enter from Manhattan so the skyline's ahead of you, and stay on the upper pedestrian path.

    EatTime Out Market · DUMBO.Dozens of vendors plus a rooftop terrace with the classic Manhattan-Bridge-and-skyline view.

    PM

    DUMBO + the adidas "Home of Soccer" fan zone at Brooklyn Bridge Park.Free with a reservation (book ~24h ahead): screens, a beer garden and a 3v3 pitch, 12–10pm. Time it to whatever match is on.

    EatJuliana's · DUMBO.Coal-oven pizza under the bridge — whole pies only (no slices); the classic Margherita, and expect a short line.

  6. 06
    Day 06Wed · Jun 24 · Heights & lights
    AM

    Grand Central + the New York Public Library + Bryant Park.Don't miss Grand Central's ceiling and the Whispering Gallery, then the NYPL's Rose Reading Room a block away — all free.

    EatGrand Central Oyster Bar · inside the terminal.A NY institution — oysters, chowder and the pan roast. Opens 11:30 and closed weekends, so Wednesday's ideal.

    PM

    SUMMIT One Vanderbilt — the mirrored-glass observation deck above Grand Central, late afternoon into dusk.Go ~1–1.5 hrs before sunset for day, golden hour and the lights; book a timed ticket. The glass Ascent elevator is a fun add-on.

    EatLos Tacos No.1 · Times Square.Counter-only, no seats, but elite — the adobada (al pastor) is the pick. Perfect before a Broadway show.

  7. 07
    Day 07Thu · Jun 25 · Village wind-down
    AM

    Greenwich Village & Washington Square Park, then the West Village brownstone streets — Bedford, Grove, Commerce.Some of the prettiest, most-filmed blocks in the city — a gentle final-day wander.

    EatJack's Wife Freda · West Village.No reservations for small parties; the green shakshuka and rosewater waffle are the favourites. A short wait is normal.

    PM

    SoHo — cast-iron architecture and shopping (Broadway, Prince, Greene St), into Nolita and Little Italy.Greene and Mercer have the best cast-iron facades — great for a last wander and gifts.

    EatBalthazar · SoHo.Grand French brasserie — the bread basket and steak frites. Book ahead on Resy, or sit at the bar (bakery next door for pastries to go).

Tip: keep your flight time in mind on Day 07 — the subway runs straight back up to Midtown when you need it.

CEvening options

Off the schedule on purpose — pick a night out whenever you fancy one.

Broadway show · Theater District
Same-day discounts at the TKTS booth in Times Square, or try the official rush / lottery apps for big titles.
Comedy Cellar · Greenwich Village
Legendary stand-up in a tiny basement; reserve ahead, two-item minimum, surprise drop-ins are common.
Jazz: Village Vanguard or Blue Note · Village
Two sets a night — arrive early for a good seat at the Vanguard's iconic basement room.
SUMMIT or Top of the Rock after dark · Midtown
The city lit up; both run late, and the queues thin out in the evening.
Rooftop drinks for sunset · Midtown
Plenty of skyline rooftops a short walk from the hotel — golden hour is prime.
Peter Luger · Williamsburg, Brooklyn
Old-school dry-aged steak, an institution — book well ahead; order one size down (the steak for two feeds three).
Katz's Delicatessen · Lower East Side
Pastrami on rye, open late — grab a ticket at the door and tip your cutter. A New York rite of passage.
A night walk · anywhere
The Brooklyn Bridge lit up or Times Square after dark — both are pure New York at night.

                  Before you go / good to know

                  Getting around. From Park Lane your closest stations are 57 ST (F) · 5 AV/59 ST (N·R·W) · 59 ST–COLUMBUS CIRCLE (A·C·B·D·1). Just tap a contactless card with OMNY — after 12 rides in one week, the rest are free.
