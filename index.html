    CEvening options

    Off the schedule on purpose — pick a night out whenever you fancy one.

    Broadway show · Theater District
    Same-day discounts at the TKTS booth in Times Square, or try the official rush / lottery apps for big titles.
    Comedy Cellar · Greenwich Village
    Legendary stand-up in a tiny basement; reserve ahead, two-item minimum, surprise drop-ins are common.
    Jazz: Village Vanguard or Blue Note · Village
    Two sets a night — arrive early for a good seat at the Vanguard's iconic basement room.
    SUMMIT or Top of the Rock after dark · Midtown
    The city lit up; both run late, and the queues thin out in the evening.
    Rooftop drinks for sunset · Midtown
    Plenty of skyline rooftops a short walk from the hotel — golden hour is prime.
    Peter Luger · Williamsburg, Brooklyn
    Old-school dry-aged steak, an institution — book well ahead; order one size down (the steak for two feeds three).
    Katz's Delicatessen · Lower East Side
    Pastrami on rye, open late — grab a ticket at the door and tip your cutter. A New York rite of passage.
    A night walk · anywhere
    The Brooklyn Bridge lit up or Times Square after dark — both are pure New York at night.

                    Before you go / good to know

                    Getting around. From Park Lane your closest stations are 57 ST (F) · 5 AV/59 ST (N·R·W) · 59 ST–COLUMBUS CIRCLE (A·C·B·D·1). Just tap a contactless card with OMNY — after 12 rides in one week, the rest are free.

                    Book before you fly. The Statue of Liberty ferry, 9/11 Museum, the Met, SUMMIT and Top of the Rock all use timed tickets that sell out — lock them in early.

                    More World Cup, free. Beyond Brooklyn Bridge Park, the Queens Fan Zone at the USTA Billie Jean King Center (Flushing) runs live screenings and concerts through 27 June.
